Ternary Operator
----------------
- The Ternary operator is a conditional operator that takes three (3) operands
    - A conditional statement is defined first
    - Followed by a ?
    - An expression to be executed if the conditional statement is truthy
    - Followed by a :
    - An expression to be executed if the conditional statement is falsy

    - Reference (https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Reference/Operators/Conditional_operator)
    
Syntax:

condition ? exprIfTrue : exprIfFalse


- The Ternary operator is often used as an ALTERNATIVE to an if..else statement
